Proposal

Problem Statement

The goal of our project is to create a model, using supervised classification, that is able to identify and diagnose nodules, masses, and cardiomegaly from an inputted Chest X-ray. A hough transformation and deformable contour will be to then locate the ailment specifically within the X-Ray. Using our system, someone can input a Chest X-ray of a patient, and our model will predict whether or not the patient has a nodule, mass, or cardiomegaly. If the X-ray diagnosis is positive, the Chest X-ray will be outputted with a rectangle encasing area of interest (the enlarged heart, nodule, or mass) and the estimated size of the nodule/mass.


Approach

We will be utilizing supervised learning to detect features indicative of a mass, nodule, or cardiomegaly. This will be done by training a convolutional neural network to extract features and classify the input X-Ray images, with a certain confidence, into the various classes representing each condition. There will be some overlap between classes due to the nature of the dataset used, but we will choose the class with the highest confidence for a given image with overlapping ground truth. 5. ...

The rectangle denoting the area of interest will be created by first applying a deformable contour to roughly the center of the image if the diagnosis was cardiomegaly (since the heart is always located in the same place). Our approach for identifying nodules and masses would be preprocessing the image to remove noise and unnecessary features like the rib cage, and then use a generalized hough transformation to locate the where roughly the nodule or mass might be and then applying a deformable contour to get the more exact boundaries (since the nodules or masses are slightly non-rigid) and potentially calculating the size of the nodule or mass. The deformable contour can also serve as a way to double check the accuracy of the results, since both cardiomegaly (enlarged heart) and nodules/masses are dependent on size in their identification.

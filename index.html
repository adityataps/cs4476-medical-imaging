Proposal

Problem Statement

The goal of our project is to create a model, using supervised classification, that is able to identify and diagnose nodules, masses, and cardiomegaly from an inputted Chest X-ray. A hough transformation and deformable contour will be to then locate the ailment specifically within the X-Ray. Using our system, someone can input a Chest X-ray of a patient, and our model will predict whether or not the patient has a nodule, mass, or cardiomegaly. If the X-ray diagnosis is positive, the Chest X-ray will be outputted with a rectangle encasing area of interest (the enlarged heart, nodule, or mass) and the estimated size of the nodule/mass.


Approach

We will be utilizing supervised learning to detect features indicative of a mass, nodule, or cardiomegaly. This will be done by training a convolutional neural network to extract features and classify the input X-Ray images, with a certain confidence, into the various classes representing each condition. There will be some overlap between classes due to the nature of the dataset used, but we will choose the class with the highest confidence for a given image with overlapping ground truth.

The general architecture of our model will consist of the following:
  • Initial pre-processing within the input layer
  • CheXNet architecture used as a basis for development of hidden layers
    • Original network has 121 layers - will be narrowed down proportionally to reflect scope of this project
    • Original network was trained on 100,000 frontal chest X-Ray images - aim to narrow this down as well depending on image choice (uncertain as explained below in the experiment sub-section)
  • Hough transform after classification for location (except for cardiomegaly detection)
  • Deformable contours to define the boundaries of the ailment

The boundary denoting the area of interest will be created by first applying a deformable contour to roughly the center-right area of the image if the diagnosis was cardiomegaly (since the heart is always located in the same place). Our approach for identifying nodules and masses would be preprocessing the image to remove noise, and then use a generalized Hough transformation to locate the roughly where the nodule or mass might be and then applying a deformable contour to get the more exact boundaries (since the nodules or masses are slightly non-rigid) and potentially calculating the size (area) of the nodule or mass using the boundary.

We will be basing the architecture for our network off the CheXNet architecture that has already been built to classify chest X-Rays.

Experiments and Results

We will be using the NIH Clinical Center's publically available Chest X-Ray dataset. The dataset is prelabeled with the associated thoracic pathology (if any) and identified as training data or testing data. We will be using TensorFlow and Keras to create a convolutional neural network and train it with the training data and test the accuracy of our model’s predictions with the testing data. As for the Hough transformation and deformable contours, we will implement based on the pseudocode from class and outside of class research. Our project will be successful if we can make a model that can diagnose nodules, masses, and cardiomegaly based on any input Chest X-ray with at least 75% accuracy and denote specifically where the ailments were detected within the X-rays. The measured accuracy will be based on the F1 matrix - a statistical accuracy measure, typically used for binary classification in machine learning. This metric was used by the CheXNet architecture cited earlier in this proposal and has been modified to be comparable for multi-classification problems. Some potential uncertainties would be differentiating between nodules and masses, because of the similarities in the shape of the two ailments, as well as the specific structure of our neural network; we may also find that one architecture works better than another in detecting the relevant illnesses. Because the dataset is so large (~112,000) and covers 14 different ailments, we also have to decide which subset of images we will use to better train and test our model. An experiment we will do is testing different neural network architectures on a smaller subset of our dataset images and finding out the accuracy of the predictions developed by the different architectures. We could use the results of this experiment to determine which architectures might work the best with our dataset and goal, which would be valuable information, since one of our uncertainties is determining which architecture would work best.

Some Terminology

Pulmonary Nodule: a small round or oval-shaped growth in the lung. It may also be called a “spot on the lung” or a “coin lesion.” Pulmonary nodules are smaller than three centimeters (around 1.2 inches) in diameter.

Lung Mass: an abnormal spot or area in the lungs that are more than 3 centimeters (cm), about 1 1/2 inches, in size.

Cardiomegaly: an abnormal enlargement of the heart.

Thoracic: a medical word for things pertaining to the thorax (chest) area of your body.

Metastasize: (typically referring to cancer) to spread to other sites in the body by metastasis.
